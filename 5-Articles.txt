🇩🇪 German Articles (Artikel)

1. Definition
--------------
 Articles are words that come before nouns to show their gender, number, and case.
 In German, articles are essential because they tell you if a noun is masculine (der), feminine (die), neuter (das), or plural (die).
 Unlike English, German articles change depending on case (nominative, accusative, dative, genitive).

2. Articles in German
----------------------
1. Definite Articles (the)
Case				Masculine (der)		Feminine (die)		Neuter (das)		Plural (die)
Nominative (subject)		der → dare → the	die → dee → the		das → dahs → the	die → dee → the
Accusative (direct object)	den → dayn → the	die → dee → the		das → dahs → the	die → dee → the
Dative (indirect object)	dem → daym → the	der → dare → the	dem → daym → the	den → dayn → the
Genitive (possession)		des → dess → of the	der → dare → of the	des → dess → of the	der → dare → of the

2. Indefinite Articles (a/an)
------------------------------

⚠️ Note: No plural form for “a/an” in German.

Case				Masculine		Feminine		Neuter			Plural
Nominative			ein → ine → a		eine → ine-uh → a	ein → ine → a	—
Accusative			einen → ine-en → a	eine → ine-uh → a	ein → ine → a	—
Dative				einem → ine-em → a	einer → ine-er → a	einem → ine-em → a	—
Genitive			eines → ine-ess → of a	einer → ine-er → of a	eines → ine-ess → of a	—

3. Usage Examples
------------------
Nominative (subject):
 Der Mann liest. → Dare mahn leest. → The man is reading.
 Accusative (direct object):
 Ich sehe den Hund. → Ish zay-uh dayn hoond. → I see the dog.
 Dative (indirect object):
 Ich gebe dem Kind ein Buch. → Ish gay-buh daym kint ine bookh. → I give the child a book.
 Genitive (possession):
 Das ist das Auto des Mannes. → Dahs ist dahs ow-to dess mahn-ess. → That is the man’s car.
________________________________________
✅ Key takeaway:
•	Articles in German always agree with the noun’s gender, number, and case.
•	Definite = the (der/die/das).
•	Indefinite = a/an (ein/eine).
•	Articles change form depending on role in the sentence (subject, object, etc.).
________________________________________
3. Rules & Notes
1.	Every noun in German has a gender — masculine, feminine, or neuter. The article shows you the gender.
o	der Mann → the man (masculine)
o	die Frau → the woman (feminine)
o	das Kind → the child (neuter)
2.	Articles also change with case (we’ll learn this later in “Cases”):
o	Nominative: der Mann (the man — subject)
o	Accusative: den Mann (the man — object)
3.	Plural nouns always use “die” in nominative case.
o	die Kinder → the children
4.	No indefinite article in plural (you cannot say “a children”).
o	Kinder → children (some / any children)
________________________________________
✅ Summary:
•	Articles in German = small words before nouns.
•	Definite → der/die/das (the).
•	Indefinite → ein/eine (a/an).
•	They show gender, number, and case.
