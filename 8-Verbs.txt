🇩🇪 6. Verbs (Verben)
📘 Definition

Verbs (Verben) are words that describe actions, states, or events.
In German, verbs are central to sentence structure — the second element in main clauses and final element in subordinate clauses.
They change their form (conjugate) according to person, number, tense, and mood.

🧠 Example:

Ich gehe → I go

Du gehst → You go

Er geht → He goes

6.1 Regular & Irregular Verbs
📘 Definition

Regular verbs (schwache Verben) follow a consistent pattern when conjugated.

Irregular verbs (starke Verben) change their stem vowel or have other irregularities.

🧩 Examples
Type	German	Pronunciation	English
Regular	spielen	shpee-len	to play
Regular	machen	mah-khen	to do / make
Irregular	gehen → ging → gegangen	gay-hen / ging / geh-gahng-en	go / went / gone
Irregular	sehen → sah → gesehen	zay-hen / zah / geh-zay-hen	see / saw / seen

⚙️ Rule:
Regular verbs form the past tense with -te (e.g., machte), while irregular verbs change the stem (e.g., ging).

6.2 Modal Verbs
📘 Definition

Modal verbs (Modalverben) express possibility, necessity, ability, permission, or desire.
They modify another verb, which appears at the end of the sentence in the infinitive.

📋 List of Modal Verbs
Verb	Pronunciation	Meaning	Example	English
dürfen	dur-fen	may / to be allowed	Ich darf gehen.	I may go.
können	kur-nen	can / to be able	Ich kann schwimmen.	I can swim.
mögen	moh-gen	to like	Ich mag Kaffee.	I like coffee.
müssen	mew-sen	must / to have to	Ich muss arbeiten.	I must work.
sollen	zo-len	should / supposed to	Du sollst lernen.	You should study.
wollen	vol-len	to want	Wir wollen essen.	We want to eat.

⚙️ Rule:
Modal verbs change the word order — the second verb (in infinitive form) moves to the end.

🧩 Example:

Ich will Deutsch lernen. → I want to learn German.

6.3 Separable & Inseparable Prefix Verbs
📘 Definition

Many German verbs are built with prefixes that change meaning.
Some prefixes separate in a sentence (go to the end), while others stay attached.

Type	Example	Pronunciation	Meaning	Example in Sentence
Separable	aufstehen	owf-shtey-en	to get up	Ich stehe früh auf. → I get up early.
Inseparable	verstehen	fair-shtey-en	to understand	Ich verstehe dich. → I understand you.

⚙️ Rule:
In the present tense, the prefix of a separable verb moves to the end of the clause.
In the past participle, separable verbs use ge- between prefix and stem (e.g., aufgestanden), while inseparable verbs don’t (e.g., verstanden).

6.4 Reflexive Verbs
📘 Definition

Reflexive verbs use a reflexive pronoun (mich, dich, sich, uns, euch, sich) and indicate that the subject and object are the same person.

🧩 Examples:

German	Pronunciation	English
Ich wasche mich.	Ikh vah-sheh mikh.	I wash myself.
Du erinnerst dich.	Doo eh-rin-erst dikh.	You remember.
Er freut sich.	Air froyt zikh.	He is glad.

⚙️ Rule:
Reflexive pronouns change with person and case (mostly accusative).

6.5 Auxiliary Verbs (Hilfsverben)
📘 Definition

Used to form compound tenses, passive voice, or subjunctive mood.

Verb	Pronunciation	Meaning	Used for
haben	hah-ben	to have	Perfect tense (most verbs)
sein	zine	to be	Movement / state-change verbs
werden	vair-den	to become / will	Future & passive voice

🧩 Examples:

Ich habe gearbeitet. → I have worked.

Ich bin gegangen. → I have gone.

Es wird gemacht. → It is being done.

6.6 Verb Conjugation
📘 Definition

Changing the verb form based on person, number, or tense.

Person	spielen (to play)	Pronunciation	English
ich	spiele	shpee-leh	I play
du	spielst	shpee-lst	you play
er/sie/es	spielt	shpee-lt	he/she/it plays
wir	spielen	shpee-len	we play
ihr	spielt	shpee-lt	you (plural) play
sie/Sie	spielen	shpee-len	they/you (formal) play
6.7 Tenses (Zeiten)
Tense	Example	Pronunciation	English
Präsens (Present)	Ich lerne.	Ikh ler-neh.	I learn.
Präteritum (Simple Past)	Ich lernte.	Ikh lern-teh.	I learned.
Perfekt (Present Perfect)	Ich habe gelernt.	Ikh hah-beh geh-lernt.	I have learned.
Plusquamperfekt (Past Perfect)	Ich hatte gelernt.	Ikh ha-teh geh-lernt.	I had learned.
Futur I (Future)	Ich werde lernen.	Ikh vair-deh ler-nen.	I will learn.
Futur II (Future Perfect)	Ich werde gelernt haben.	Ikh vair-deh geh-lernt hah-ben.	I will have learned.

⚙️ Rule:
German commonly uses Perfekt in speech and Präteritum in writing.

6.8 Passive Voice (Passiv)
📘 Definition

Used to emphasize the action rather than the subject. Formed with werden + past participle.

🧩 Example:

Das Haus wird gebaut. → The house is being built.

Der Brief wurde geschrieben. → The letter was written.

⚙️ Rule:
sein + worden is used for perfect passive.

Das Haus ist gebaut worden. → The house has been built.

6.9 Subjunctive Mood (Konjunktiv I & II)
📘 Definition

Used for reported speech, hypothetical, or polite situations.

Type	Example	Pronunciation	English
Konjunktiv I (reported)	Er sagte, er sei müde.	Air zahk-teh, air zye myoo-deh.	He said he was tired.
Konjunktiv II (hypothetical/polite)	Ich würde gehen, wenn ich Zeit hätte.	Ikh vur-deh gay-en ven ikh tsyt heh-teh.	I would go if I had time.
6.10 Imperative Mood (Befehlsform)
📘 Definition

Used to give commands or requests.

Subject	Example	Pronunciation	English
du	Geh!	Gay!	Go!
ihr	Geht!	Gayt!	Go (you all)!
Sie	Gehen Sie!	Gay-hen zee!	Go (formal)!
6.11 Rules & Exceptions

Sein, haben, and werden are irregular in all tenses.

Some verbs take sein in perfect tense (movement or change of state).

Ich bin gegangen. → I have gone.

Modal verbs take haben in compound tenses.

In subordinate clauses, verbs go to the end.

Ich weiß, dass du kommst. → I know that you’re coming.

Some strong verbs have mixed patterns, e.g., bringen → brachte → gebracht.

✅ Summary

Type	Key Function	Example
Regular Verbs	Follow consistent endings	machen → machte → gemacht
Irregular Verbs	Change stem vowel	sehen → sah → gesehen
Modal Verbs	Add meaning to another verb	Ich kann schwimmen.
Separable Verbs	Prefix splits off	Ich rufe dich an.
Reflexive Verbs	Use reflexive pronouns	Ich wasche mich.
Auxiliary Verbs	Build tenses	haben / sein / werden
Passive Voice	Action-focused	Das Haus wird gebaut.
Subjunctive	Hypothetical or polite	Ich würde gehen.
Imperative	Commands	Geh! / Gehen Sie!
