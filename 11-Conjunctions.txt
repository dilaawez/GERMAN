🇩🇪 German Grammar: Conjunctions (Konjunktionen)
================================================

🧠 Definition

Conjunctions are words that connect sentences, phrases, or words — just like in English words such as and, because, although, or either...or.

In German, conjunctions help you combine thoughts logically.

German word: Konjunktionen
Pronunciation (English): Kon-yoonk-tsyo-nen


1️⃣ Coordinating Conjunctions (Nebenordnende Konjunktionen)
-----------------------------------------------------------

🧠 Definition

They connect equal parts of a sentence — for example, two main clauses or two words.
The word order does not change after them.

🧩 Common Coordinating Conjunctions
German		Pronunciation (English)		English Meaning
und		oond				and
aber		ah-ber				but
oder		oh-der				or
denn		den				because / for
sondern		zon-dern			but rather / instead

💬 Examples

Ich trinke Kaffee und er trinkt Tee.
(Eekh trink-uh kah-fay oond air trinkt tay)
→ I drink coffee and he drinks tea.

Er ist müde, aber er arbeitet.
(Air ist myoo-duh, ah-ber air ar-bye-tet)
→ He is tired, but he is working.

Ich esse kein Fleisch, sondern Gemüse.
(Eekh es-se kïne flysh, zon-dern guh-myoo-zeh)
→ I don’t eat meat, but rather vegetables.

🧩 Easy Tip:
Coordinating conjunctions don’t change word order — the verb stays in second position in both parts of the sentence.


2️⃣ Subordinating Conjunctions (Unterordnende Konjunktionen)
------------------------------------------------------------

🧠 Definition

These connect a main clause and a dependent clause (a clause that cannot stand alone).
They send the verb to the end of the dependent clause.

🧩 Common Subordinating Conjunctions
German		Pronunciation (English)	English Meaning
weil		vyle			because
dass		dass			that
wenn		ven			if / when
obwohl		ob-vohl			although
damit		dah-mit			so that
bevor		beh-for			before
nachdem		nahkh-daym		after

💬 Examples

Ich bleibe zu Hause, weil es regnet.
(Eekh bly-buh tsoo how-zeh, vyle es rayg-net)
→ I’m staying home because it’s raining.

Er sagt, dass er keine Zeit hat.
(Air zahkt, dass air kÿ-nuh tsyt haht)
→ He says that he has no time.

Ich gehe ins Kino, obwohl ich müde bin.
(Eekh gay-uh ins kee-no, ob-vohl eekh myoo-duh bin)
→ I’m going to the cinema although I’m tired.

🧩 Easy Tip:
When you use a subordinating conjunction — the verb moves to the end of that clause.
👉 Example: weil es regnet → “because it rains.”


3️⃣ Correlative Conjunctions (Doppelte Konjunktionen)
----------------------------------------------------

🧠 Definition

These come in pairs — they connect two ideas that belong together, similar to English either...or, both...and, not only...but also.

🧩 Common Correlative Conjunctions
German				Pronunciation (English)		English Meaning
entweder ... oder		ent-vay-der ... oh-der		either ... or
sowohl ... als auch		zo-vohl ... alts owkh		both ... and
nicht nur ... sondern auch	nikht noor ... zon-dern owkh	not only ... but also
weder ... noch			vay-der ... nokh		neither ... nor

💬 Examples

Entweder du kommst, oder du bleibst.
(Ent-vay-der doo komst, oh-der doo blypst)
→ Either you come or you stay.

Sowohl Peter als auch Anna sind hier.
(Zo-vohl pay-ter alts owkh ah-nah zint heer)
→ Both Peter and Anna are here.

Er ist nicht nur klug, sondern auch fleißig.
(Air ist nikht noor kloog, zon-dern owkh fly-sikh)
→ He is not only smart but also hardworking.

🧩 Easy Tip:
Correlative conjunctions always come in pairs — the first half goes before one clause or word, and the second half before the other.

🧭 Summary Table
Type		Function				Word Order		Examples
Coordinating	Connects equal parts			No change		und, aber, oder
Subordinating	Connects main + dependent clause	Verb goes to end	weil, dass, obwohl
Correlative	Works in pairs				No change		entweder...oder, nicht nur...sondern auch


✅ In Short:

Coordinating → joins equal ideas (verb stays second).

Subordinating → introduces a reason or condition (verb to the end).

Correlative → joins two equal ideas with paired words.
