🇩🇪 5. Adjectives (Adjektive)
=============================

📘 Definition

Adjectives (Adjektive) are words that describe nouns — they tell you what kind, which one, or how something is.

In German, adjectives change their endings depending on the gender, number, and case of the noun they describe.

🧠 Example:

ein schöner Tag → “a beautiful day”

eine schöne Frau → “a beautiful woman”

ein schönes Haus → “a beautiful house”

-------------------------------------

schön		shern			beautiful

groß		gross (like “gross” in English but with longer ‘o’)	big / tall

🧱 Position in a Sentence
---------------------------
Adjectives in German can appear in two main forms:

Attributive adjectives → come before the noun and take endings.

Example: ein schöner Hund → a beautiful dog

The ending (-er) depends on the article and case.


Predicate adjectives → come after the verb (usually sein = to be) and don’t take endings.

Example: Der Hund ist schön. → The dog is beautiful.

⚙️ Adjective Endings (Basics)
-----------------------------
German adjectives change their endings depending on:

Gender: masculine, feminine, neuter

Case: nominative, accusative, dative, genitive

Article type: definite (der/die/das) or indefinite (ein/eine/ein)

Example:

Article	Noun	Adjective	Translation
der	Hund	große Hund	the big dog
die	Katze	kleine Katze	the small cat
das	Haus	alte Haus	the old house
ein	Hund	großer Hund	a big dog
eine	Blume	schöne Blume	a beautiful flower

🧠 Rule Summary
----------------
Concept	Rule								Example
Adjectives before nouns	Must take endings				Ein schöner Tag
Adjectives after “sein”	No endings					Der Tag ist schön.
Endings depend on gender & case	Masculine, feminine, neuter, plural	Ein kleiner Hund / Eine kleine Katze
Always lowercase	Adjectives aren’t capitalized in German		ein rotes Auto
