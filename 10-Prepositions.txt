🇩🇪 8.8. Prepositions (Präpositionen)
=====================================

📘 Definition

Prepositions (Präpositionen) are small words that show the relationship between nouns or pronouns and other parts of a sentence — for example: place, direction, time, or possession.

In English, these are words like in, on, under, with, from, to, about.

In German, each preposition affects the case (Akkusativ, Dativ, Genitiv) of the noun that follows it.

🧠 Example idea:

English: “The book is on the table.”
German: “Das Buch liegt auf dem Tisch.”


⚙️ 1️⃣ Accusative Prepositions (Akkusativpräpositionen)
-----------------------------------------------------

These prepositions always take the accusative case — used mostly for movement or direction (where to?).

🔹 Common Accusative Prepositions:

German		Pronunciation		English
durch		doorkh			through
für		fyoor			for
gegen		gay-gen			against
ohne		oh-neh			without
um		oom			around / at (time)

💬 Examples:

Ich gehe durch den Park.
Ikh gay-he doorkh den park. → I walk through the park.

Das Geschenk ist für dich.
Das geh-shenk ist fyoor dikh. → The gift is for you.

Er fährt ohne seine Freunde.
Air fairt oh-neh zy-neh froin-deh. → He travels without his friends.


⚙️ 2️⃣ Dative Prepositions (Dativpräpositionen)
---------------------------------------------

These prepositions always take the dative case — used for location, position, or association (where at?).

🔹 Common Dative Prepositions:

German		Pronunciation		English
aus		ows			out of / from
bei		bye			at / near / with
mit		mit			with
nach		nahkh			after / to (city or country)
seit		zyt			since / for (time)
von		fon			from / of
zu		tsoo			to

💬 Examples:

Ich komme aus Deutschland.
Ikh kom-meh ows doytch-lahnd. → I come from Germany.

Er wohnt bei seinen Eltern.
Air vohnt bye zy-nen el-tarn. → He lives with / near his parents.

Wir fahren mit dem Bus.
Veer fahr-en mit dehm boos. → We go by bus.


⚙️ 3️⃣ Genitive Prepositions (Genitivpräpositionen)
-------------------------------------------------

These prepositions always take the genitive case — often used in formal or written German, showing possession or relation.

🔹 Common Genitive Prepositions:

German		Pronunciation		English
(an)statt	(ahn-shtat)		instead of
trotz		trots			despite
während		vehr-rent		during
wegen		vay-gen			because of

💬 Examples:

Trotz des Regens gehen wir spazieren.
Trots des ray-gens gay-en veer shpa-tsee-ren. → We go for a walk despite the rain.

Wegen des Staus komme ich zu spät.
Vay-gen des shtows kom-me ikh tsoo shpayt. → I am late because of the traffic jam.

Anstatt des Kaffees trinke ich Tee.
Ahn-shtat des kah-fehs trin-keh ikh tay. → Instead of coffee, I drink tea.


⚙️ 4️⃣ Two-Way Prepositions (Wechselpräpositionen)
------------------------------------------------

These prepositions can take either accusative (movement) or dative (location) — depending on whether the action shows motion or position.

�� Common Two-Way Prepositions:

German		Pronunciation		English
an		ahn			at / on (vertical surface)
auf		owf			on / onto (horizontal surface)
hinter		hin-ter			behind
in		in			in / into
neben		nay-ben			next to
über		oo-ber			over / above
unter		oon-ter			under
vor		for			in front of
zwischen	tsvish-en		between

💬 Examples (Accusative = movement):

Ich gehe in die Schule.
Ikh gay-he in dee shoo-leh. → I go into the school.

Er legt das Buch auf den Tisch.
Air laygt das book owf den tish. → He puts the book onto the table.

💬 Examples (Dative = location):

Das Buch liegt auf dem Tisch.
Das book leegt owf dehm tish. → The book is on the table.

Der Hund schläft unter dem Bett.
Dair hoont shlaeft oon-ter dehm bet. → The dog sleeps under the bed.


🧩 5️⃣ Prepositional Phrases (Präpositionalphrasen)
--------------------------------------------------

These are preposition + noun combinations that act as a unit to describe where, when, or how something happens.

💬 Examples:

Im Büro (in dem Büro) – im boo-roh → in the office

Am Wochenende (an dem Wochenende) – ahm vokh-en-en-deh → on the weekend

Bei mir – bye meer → at my place


🧩 6️⃣ Fixed Expressions with Prepositions (Feste Verbindungen)
--------------------------------------------------------------

Certain verbs, adjectives, or expressions always require specific prepositions — these must be memorized.

German Expression	Pronunciation	English Translation
warten auf (Akk.)	vahr-ten owf	to wait for
sprechen mit (Dat.)	shprekh-en mit	to speak with
denken an (Akk.)	denk-en ahn	to think of
sich freuen über (Akk.)	zikh froin ooh-ber	to be happy about
Angst haben vor (Dat.)	ahngst hah-ben for	to be afraid of

💬 Example Sentences:

Ich warte auf den Bus.
Ikh vahr-te owf den boos. → I’m waiting for the bus.

Sie spricht mit ihrem Lehrer.
Zee shprikht mit eer-em lay-rer. → She is speaking with her teacher.

Er denkt an seine Freunde.
Air denkt ahn zy-neh froin-deh. → He is thinking of his friends.

⚙️ Quick Summary Table
Case Type		Question Answered		Example					Translation
Accusative		Wohin? (Where to?)		Ich gehe durch den Park.		I go through the park.
Dative			Wo? (Where at?)			Ich bin bei meinen Freunden.		I am with my friends.
Genitive		Wessen? (Whose / Because of?)	Wegen des Wetters bleibe ich zu Hause.	I’m staying home because of the weather.
Two-Way	Wohin / Wo	Ich lege das Buch auf den Tisch. / Das Buch liegt auf dem Tisch.	I put the book on the table. / The book is on the table.

✅ In Short:

Prepositions in German govern cases and define relationships of place, time, direction, and reason.
Always check whether the preposition is accusative, dative, genitive, or two-way, since it changes the article form.
