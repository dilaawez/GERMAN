🇩🇪 German Nouns (Die Substantive)
=================================
1. What is a Noun?

A noun names a person, place, thing, or idea.

In German:

All nouns are capitalized.

Each noun has a gender:

der = masculine

die = feminine

das = neuter

# Nouns also change depending on number (singular/plural) and case (nominative, accusative, dative, genitive).

2. Pronunciation of Articles

der → like “dare”

die → like “dee”

das → like “dahs”

3. Examples by Gender

Masculine Nouns (der)
---------------------
Often end in -er, -en, -el.

German		    Pronunciation (English sounds)	English
der Mann	    dare mahn	                    the man
der Hund	    dare hoond	                    the dog
der Tisch	    dare tish	                    the table
der Lehrer	    dare LAY-rer	                the teacher
der Apfel	    dare AHP-fel	                the apple


Feminine Nouns (die)
--------------------
Often end in -e, -heit, -keit, -ung.

German		    Pronunciation (English sounds)	English
die Frau	    dee frow (rhymes with cow)	    the woman
die Katze	    dee KAHT-tseh	                the cat
die Blume	    dee BLOO-meh	                the flower
die Freiheit    dee FRY-hite	                freedom
die Zeitung	    dee TSI-tung	                the newspaper

Neuter Nouns (das)
------------------
Often end in -chen, -lein, -ment, -um.

German		    Pronunciation (English sounds)	English
das Kind	    dahs kint	                    the child
das Buch	    dahs bookh (like book with throaty ‘kh’)	the book
das Mädchen	    dahs MAYT-chen	                the girl
das Auto	    dahs OW-toh	                    the car
das Zentrum	    dahs TSEHN-trum	                the center

4. Sentence Examples

Der Mann liest ein Buch.
Pronunciation: Dare mahn leest ine bookh
Translation: The man is reading a book.

Die Frau fährt ein Auto.
Pronunciation: Dee frow fehrt ine ow-toh
Translation: The woman drives a car.

Das Kind spielt im Haus.
Pronunciation: Dahs kint shpeelt im house
Translation: The child plays in the house.

Die Katze schläft auf dem Tisch.
Pronunciation: Dee KAHT-tseh shlaeft owf dehm tish
Translation: The cat sleeps on the table.

Der Hund läuft durch die Stadt.
Pronunciation: Dare hoond loyft doorkh dee shtat
Translation: The dog runs through the city.

5. Quick Gender Rules with Examples

Masculine (der): often -er, -en, -el

der Lehrer (teacher), der Wagen (car), der Apfel (apple)

Feminine (die): often -e, -heit, -keit, -ung

die Blume (flower), die Freiheit (freedom), die Zeitung (newspaper)

Neuter (das): often -chen, -lein, -ment, -um

das Mädchen (girl), das Instrument (instrument), das Zentrum (center)

⚠️ Note: These are patterns, but there are exceptions. Always learn nouns with their article.

6. Practice Exercises

A. Identify the gender (der/die/das):

___ Hund (dog)

___ Blume (flower)

___ Mädchen (girl)

___ Zeitung (newspaper)

___ Auto (car)

B. Fill in the blank:

___ Mann liest ein Buch. (The man reads a book.)

Die ___ ist schön. (The flower is beautiful.)

Der Hund frisst ___. (the cat)

Ich fahre ___. (the car)

___ spielt draußen. (the child)

7. Answers

A.

der Hund

die Blume

das Mädchen

die Zeitung

das Auto

B.

Der Mann liest ein Buch.

Die Blume ist schön.

Der Hund frisst die Katze.

Ich fahre das Auto.

Das Kind spielt draußen.
